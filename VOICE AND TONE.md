# VOICE & TONALITY GUIDELINES

## Core Principle
Communicate with clarity, confidence, and control.

The tone should feel:
- intentional
- composed
- high-value

Avoid anything that feels forced, exaggerated, or generic.

---

## Tone Characteristics

- Confident, not loud  
- Direct, not aggressive  
- Clear, not complex  
- Refined, not flashy  
- Modern, not trendy  

---

## Voice Style

Write as if speaking to a capable, intelligent reader.

- Use straightforward language
- Keep sentences purposeful
- Avoid unnecessary filler
- Maintain a calm, controlled delivery

---

## Communication Rules

- Prioritize clarity over cleverness  
- Keep wording simple and readable  
- Remove anything that doesn’t add value  
- Avoid over-explaining  

---

## Language Guidelines

### Use:
- Clean, precise wording  
- Neutral, professional phrasing  
- Consistent structure  

### Avoid:
- Buzzwords (e.g. “innovative,” “dynamic,” “cutting-edge”)  
- Overly promotional language  
- Clichés or filler phrases  
- Excessive punctuation or emphasis  

---

## Structure

- Keep content easy to scan  
- Use spacing intentionally  
- Break up dense text  
- Maintain consistent formatting  

---

## Consistency

The voice should remain stable across:
- headlines  
- body text  
- buttons  
- supporting content  

No sudden shifts in tone.

---

## Final Check

Before finalizing, ensure:

- The message is clear and easy to understand  
- The tone feels controlled and professional  
- Nothing feels exaggerated or unnecessary  

If it reads clean and intentional, it’s on brand.
